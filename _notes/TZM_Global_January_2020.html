Mark Enoch: 
    Updating the FCN directory in changingtheworldiseasy.com.  I have found multiple sites that are promoting similar ideas that have many great links to projects and FCNs and I was delighted to discover that there are more than 10 thousand FCNs active and growing around the world, including more than 10M users. Multiple apps are functional for freecycling, Freecycle, Trashnothing, Freegle, Telodoygratis, etc. And the Buy Nothing Project is working on an app that would integrate most kinds of free collaboration, facilitating not only freecycling but also sharing of all kind. https://www.soop.app/   "soop will be an online platform and mobile app that makes resources accessible to everyone equally, at no cost, and without limitation"
    Another great news is that it is becoming popular for public libraries in the USA to become full fledged resource banks, offering instruments, tools, electronic devices, games, single transportation, fully free of cost!  This is a gigantic leap towards our goal! 
    I have been giving many surprise speeches in the subway, speeking against the profit motive and teaching how to promote generosity and free collaboration instead. I have been getting many aplauses and thanks, people from public administration also encouraged me to continue. 
    I distributed 1000 flyers in mailboxes in apartment buildings by asking to be allowed in to distribute community information. 
    I have contacted a selectman of my town. He will organize a meeting with me and the public library to propose they become a full fledged resource bank. I also asked to be able to give his party a talk about Prosocializing (creating a shared world) 
    I met with an activist of Barcelona to introduce her to us and we are thinking about organizing a Zday with other activist groups. 
    I gave a Prosocialization talk to the Rotary club of the town next door (30 people), they loved it. 
    I am getting more and more used to saying "Happy Gift Society " instead of good bye. 
    I have been doing my best to do the hand sign C when people take photos of me.
